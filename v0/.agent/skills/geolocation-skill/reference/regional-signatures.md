# Regional Signature Patterns

Quick reference for distinctive regional combinations.

## Europe

### Nordic Countries
- **Common**: Clean road markings, modern infrastructure, wooden houses, birch/pine forests
- **Sweden**: Yellow background road signs, specific bollard style, Volvo/Saab presence
- **Norway**: Dramatic fjord terrain, tunnels, distinctive road number signs (white on blue)
- **Finland**: Straight roads through forests, bilingual signs (Finnish/Swedish in coastal areas)
- **Denmark**: Flat terrain, wind turbines, brick buildings, cycle infrastructure

### British Isles
- **UK**: Left driving, white road markings, hedgerows, distinctive phone boxes, "SLOW" road markings
- **Ireland**: Green road signs with Irish language, different bollard style from UK

### Mediterranean
- **Spain**: Kilometer markers, yellow license plates, terracotta roofs
- **Italy**: Green autostrada signs, Vespa/Fiat presence, specific guardrail style
- **Greece**: Blue/white aesthetic, Greek script, mountainous terrain
- **Portugal**: Azulejo tiles, distinctive cobblestone patterns (cal√ßada)

### Central Europe
- **Germany**: Autobahn style, specific road sign design, orderly infrastructure
- **France**: Distinctive blue road signs, plane trees along roads, pharmacie crosses
- **Netherlands**: Flat, cycle paths, windmills, water management infrastructure
- **Switzerland**: Multilingual signs, tunnel infrastructure, mountain backdrop

### Eastern Europe
- **Poland**: Specific road sign style, mix of old/new infrastructure
- **Czechia**: Tram networks, distinctive architecture mix
- **Russia**: Cyrillic signage, wide roads, specific utility pole style

## Asia

### East Asia
- **Japan**: Complex signage (kanji/hiragana/katakana), vending machines, meticulous infrastructure
- **South Korea**: Hangul signage, modern infrastructure, specific apartment style
- **China**: Simplified characters, distinctive green/blue highway signs, surveillance infrastructure
- **Taiwan**: Traditional characters, scooter prevalence, tropical vegetation in south

### Southeast Asia
- **Thailand**: Thai script, Buddhist temples, tropical vegetation, tuk-tuks
- **Vietnam**: Long country shape, French colonial influence, dense scooter traffic
- **Indonesia**: Islamic influence, tropical vegetation, distinctive minibus (angkot)
- **Malaysia**: Mix of Malay/Chinese/Tamil signage, Islamic and Chinese architecture
- **Singapore**: Dense urban, multilingual (English primary), extremely clean

### South Asia
- **India**: Devanagari and English, colorful trucks, diverse architecture
- **Nepal**: Mountain terrain, distinctive temple architecture

## Americas

### North America
- **USA**: State-specific signs, imperial measurements, diverse landscapes by region
- **Canada**: Bilingual (English/French) in some regions, metric, specific highway markers
- **Mexico**: Spanish, distinctive road style, colonial architecture in cities

### South America
- **Brazil**: Portuguese, tropical to temperate range, distinctive favela architecture
- **Argentina**: Spanish, European-influenced architecture, pampas grassland
- **Chile**: Long/narrow country, Andes mountains, wine regions

## Oceania

### Australia
- **Distinctive**: Left driving, kangaroo/wildlife warning signs, vast distances, red earth interior
- **State variants**: Different license plate colors by state

### New Zealand
- **Distinctive**: Green landscapes, sheep, Maori placenames, similar to UK but more rugged

## Middle East & Africa

### Middle East
- **Common**: Arabic script, desert/arid climate, Islamic architecture
- **UAE/Gulf**: Modern infrastructure, distinctive skylines
- **Israel**: Hebrew script, security infrastructure

### Africa
- **South Africa**: English/Afrikaans signs, distinctive infrastructure
- **North Africa**: Arabic/French influence, Mediterranean climate in coastal areas
- **Sub-Saharan**: Variable infrastructure quality, distinctive vegetation patterns by region
